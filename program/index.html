Keynote Speakers

Roger Beaty

Assistant Professor of Psychology
Penn State University

Roger Beaty is an assistant professor in the Department of Psychology at Penn State University. His research delves into the neural and cognitive underpinnings of creative thinking, utilizing tools like fMRI and neuromodulation (e.g., tES-fNIRS) to explore brain network dynamics and causally test neurocognitive mechanisms. By linking brain activity during creative tasks to processes like memory, attention, and cognitive control, and developing open-access resources for measuring creativity, his work aims to enhance creativity across domains like musical improvisation and scientific problem-solving. After earning a BA in psychology at Temple University, a PhD at University of North Carolina at Greensboro (UNCG) with Paul Silvia, and completing postdoctoral training in cognitive neuroscience at Harvard University with Daniel Schacter, he joined Penn State’s Department of Psychology in 2018 as an assistant professor.

Lorraine (Xiang) Li

Assistant Professor of Computer Science
University of Pittsburgh

Xiang Lorraine Li is an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Pittsburgh. Her research interests lie at the intersection of natural language processing and machine learning, in particular, she's interested in understanding model behavior via evaluation benchmark design and exploration around the meaning of model parameters in complex or long-tail situations, for example, high-impact domains such as law and education. By doing this, she aims to construct socially responsible, equitable, and robust models that cater to diverse users, populations, cultures, and scenarios. Her research plan on building puristic models is presented in the AAAI new faculty highlight in 2025, and he work has been published in NLP and ML conferences. She worked as a young investigator with Yejin Choi at AI2 before joining Pitt. Previously, she defended her Ph.D. in Computer Science from UMass Amherst in August 2022, working with Andrew McCallum.
